  • Business modeling

    Using standard design processes such as Canvas, you come to identify the most effective strategy for introducing the desired innovation in your business.

    The design involves context analysis, requirements definition, value proposition modeling, customer understanding, revenue streams selection, patterns selection, and so on.

    The outcome of the design phase will describe, up to Pitch Deck and Business Plan, the new innovative business model, appropriate to the challenges of the Digital Economy.

  • High-level design

    Each business model for the Digital Age is based on a specific computer system. It automates the operational process that produces the value proposition.

    Using standard software design techniques and tools, it is possible define requirements and design the business flow of the software application that meets the business model.

    Outcome of this phase is documentation for the management and technical documentation and files that can be used immediately for software development.

  • Project management

    Implementing a new business model is a complex process that needs to be handled strictly. It is good practice to design in detail the start-up process in order to be able to quickly trigger the expected revenue streams and not raise investment costs.

    The management of the start-up project must be entrusted to staff with deep experience, capable of steering all phases, developing and deploying software, and harmonizing IT resources. From designing and monitoring the results of the marketing plan, to the monitoring of digital communication.

  • Mentoring

    Resistance to change is an obstacle to innovation.

    To reduce the risk of failure of innovation projects, the widely used method is the one based on knowledge transfer and sharing.

    Both in the cases of introducing new business models within pre-existing firm and start-ups created specifically around a specific business model, the mentoring activity is best suited to climate improvement, cracking down on mistrust and minimizing cultural gaps.

«Ontology of Esperanto» Research Project

The project «Ontology of Esperanto» is proposed as one of the first attempts to produce a formal representation of a non-formal language (natural language), based on the Semantic Web standards.

«An OWL Ontology of a Sumerian Grammar» Research Project

The outcomes of the project «An OWL Ontology of a Sumerian Grammar» demonstrate how it is possible to apply the technologies of the Semantic Web also to formlize the grammar of an ancient natural language.

«Dhi Qar Knowledge Base» Research Project

«Dhi Qar Knowledge Base» is the name of the software development project performed in 2007-2008, aimed to create an innovative Knowledge-driven System designed for cataloguing archaeological and epigraphic finds.
